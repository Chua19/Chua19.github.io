Glen's idea in teaching public speaking is to ensure that students have robust opportunities to fill and try again with the support of community and meaningful and compelling feedback. By doing so students feel a sense of empowerment, personal growth, and elevated sense of self agency. Glenn's insights into teaching have been award-winning and validated by his 26 years of teaching experience, publications of his ideas, and designation as an HP Teaching Fellow and TEDx talk.

About Glen

Glen Coleman has been teaching social studies at a public high school in North Jersey for twenty-six years. His doctoral dissesrtation focused on student-centered learning. In 2019, Glen was named an HP Teaching Fellow. His publications include articles for the NJEA Review and Digital Promise and a TEDx talk, To Teach Well, Let 'Em Fail. He hosts the podcast Teacher, Breathe, and is the author of 100 or Nothing: Reimagining Success in the Classroom: a love letter to teachers that encourages all of us to rethink the possibilities of the classroom. Bill Librera and Bret Schundler, former NJ Commissioners of Education, hail Glen's approach as innovative.

Boost students' conversational fluency

with course material, build confidence in personal expression, and hone research skills
with "Two-Minutes to Master", a program that encourages students to deliver two-minute speeches
without notes on topics that tie course content to current issues. Teachers will learn how to
create a safe and supportive workshop setting, craft compelling prompts, rethink classroom architecture,
and leverage the power of second and third attempts at mastery in order to build a culture of growth and inclusivity.

  • One Hour Workshop

    Teachers will learn how to:

      Help students deliver cocmpelling two-minute speeches with no notes, i.e. Two-Minutes to Mastery.
      Create a supportive workshop environment for students.
      Craft compelling prompts, arguments, and value specific facts and focus.
      Appreciate the power of creating a classroom culture where failure (i.e. falling short of expectations) is seen as an opportunity to grow and try again. In short, when the bar is high and the stakes are low good things happen.

  • Half Day Workshop

    In addition to One Hour Workshop, teachers will learn how to:

      Help students deliver compelling two-minute speeches with no notes, i.e. Two-Minutes to Mastery.
      Write energizing prompts, craft concise arguments, and deliver lucid speeches.
      "Fail" and try again in collaboration with colleagues as teachers will deliver "One-Minute Speeches" no notes.

  • Full Day Workshop

    In addition to One and Half Day Workshops, teachers will learn how to:

      Clarify expectations for Two-Minutes to Mastery in a workshop environment.
      Help students research with specific focus to help them demonstrate fluent command.
      Help students further support other students to try again.
